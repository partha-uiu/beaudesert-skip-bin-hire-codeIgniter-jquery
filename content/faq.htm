Skip bins are great for General Household and Garage clean ups, General Garden clean ups, Moving house, Building renovation and Landscaping waste. The bin hire period is 7 days. (Extra days are available at the time of booking)

Materials we DO NOT accept are liquid waste, dangerous chemicals, acids and poisons, needles and syringes, Explosives, asbestos and any other environmentally unfriendly substances or objects likely to cause harm or damage.

Truck Tyres and Industrial tyres are not accepted under any circumstances, please do not place these in the bin, *Heavy penalties will apply*

Materials we DO Accept

A Tyre that is mounted on a rim

Car tyres can be disposed of if declaring the quantity and paying the extra fees at the time of booking *You can only dispose of the maximum allowable per bin*

Gas bottles can be disposed of if declaring the quantity and paying the extra fees at the time of booking *You can only dispose of the maximum allowable per bin* GAS BOTTLES MUST BE EMPTY OF ALL GAS

General Waste skips can be used for most all wastes such as greenery, household and scrap metal, furniture, white goods, cardboard, plastic, or a mixture of these wastes. The bin hire period is 7-days. (Extra days are available at the time of booking)

Green Waste Bin

Green waste can contain palm fronds, tree trimmings gardening waste, grass clippings or organic waste. Please no sand, soil, rocks, tree or palm trunks, or heavy waste materials.(Green Waste Only) The bin hire period is 7-days. (Extra days are available at the time of booking)


Solid Waste Bin

Concrete, soil, clay, rocks, bricks, timber, construction/demolition waste, The bin hire period is 7-days. (Extra days are available at the time of booking)

Scrap Metal Bin

Scrap Metal Only ( No Exceptions ) The bin hire period is 7-days. (Extra days are available at the time of booking)

Transport Law

Skips are not to be loaded higher than the rim. All waste to be fully contained within the skips capacity and below its rim. There is no exception to this rule under transport law. Overloaded skips are not lifted and extra charges are applied in the event of such overloads.
